# 🛍️ The Louis Vuitton Index

### *Reading a nation's books by counting its boutiques*

_What 69,000 mall shops know about economic complexity that GDP won't tell you.
A plain-language tour of [the residual](./RESIDUAL.md) and [the link](./RESEARCH_LINK.md)._

---

**1. Can you measure how rich a place is without any income data — just by looking at what's in its malls?**

Yes, surprisingly well. A magnitude-free index built only from *which* shops exist
(led by the count of distinct luxury houses present) correlates **+0.51 with
GDP-per-capita** and **+0.81 with export complexity**. Shelves are a legible
second set of national accounts.

**2. Does a mall full of luxury imports mean the country is good at making things?**

No — and that's the whole point. It measures **demand / affluence**, not
**productive capability**. The two come apart whenever rents, remittances, or
wealth (not exports) pay for the basket. The gap between them is the signal, not
noise.

**3. If you subtract "what a country can make" from "what its malls suggest it can afford," what's left?**

The **residual** — the rentier / entrepôt / remittance index. It's a retail-side
re-derivation of Hausmann's complexity–income gap, built from boutiques instead
of trade statistics.

**4. Which country does the method flag hardest as living off rents rather than know-how?**

**Australia.** Knowing nothing but mall directories, it lands as a textbook
resource-rentier: one of the rich world's *least* complex export economies
(ECI −0.82) with retail far more affluent than that — and the gap sits exactly on
top of the sample's highest resource rents (**13.4% of GDP**). The method
re-discovers the mining economy from shop windows.

**5. Is there a country where the shelves and the export complexity simply agree?**

**Thailand** — the control case. Residual ≈ 0. Its electronics / auto export
complexity (ECI 0.89) and its retail affluence tell the same story. Nothing to
explain away; productive capability and consumption are in equilibrium.

**6. Why does the single richest country (Singapore) score *below* the line?**

Because its export complexity is even more extreme than its (deep) luxury market.
Singapore has the region's most distinct maisons (**54**) yet a world-**#1** ECI
of 2.52 — even peak consumption can't out-rank that. It's the calibration
outlier: an economy whose *complexity*, not its shopping, is the anomaly.

**7. What's the biggest blind spot — where does the method fail?**

The **remittance road**. The Philippines (8.7% of GDP in remittances) *should*
show remittance-funded affluence but reads consumption-*poor* instead — almost
certainly because its malls were partly name-only scrapes that undercount luxury.
The rentier tail is robust; the remittance tail is coverage-limited. An honest
weak spot, flagged loudly.

**8. What luxury signal survives messy, half-classified scraped data best?**

**Brand *variety*** (how many distinct maisons), not density or breadth. It's
detected on store names, so a "Louis Vuitton" counts even when its category field
is blank — and it's a diversity measure, immune to the heartland-mall dilution
that punishes Singapore for having 58 suburban malls.

**9. When a country's roadmap reaches furthest beyond its actual tech base, who is it — and is that smart?**

**Indonesia.** Thinnest revealed frontier (1.33/5), *largest* ambition gap
(+0.33), rentier affluence — and a nickel-downstreaming roadmap that's an
explicit bet to convert resource rents into EV-battery and semiconductor
capability. Foresight or over-reach is genuinely undecided; it's the most
interesting cell in the table.

**10. What's the one sentence a policymaker should take away?**

**The residual and the roadmap ambition-gap are two independent measurements of
the same thing — the distance between what a place *buys* and what it can
*make*.** Where they agree (Vietnam: aligned consumption, zero gap), the
development path is coherent; where they diverge (Indonesia), the strategy is
carrying weight the productive structure can't yet bear — and the *diffusion
clock* is what to watch.
